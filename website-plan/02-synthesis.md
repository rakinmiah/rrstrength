# Phase 2 — Synthesis

## Brand truths to preserve (from Phase 1A)
1. Authentic first-person coach voice ("I wanted to look good. The muscle was a byproduct.") — rare honesty in the category.
2. The crossed-barbell + "RR_STRENGTH" mark and dark, athletic identity.
3. Real competitive pedigree (Junior Nationals podium; IPF/SBD-level) backed by genuine event photography.
4. The money-back promise — a risk-reversal the category mostly lacks.
5. Charity/community character (Ramadan Rumble for children in Palestine) — emotional differentiation.

## Brand debts to shed (from Phase 1A)
1. Inherited filler copy ("Facilitating your growth", "A coach you can trust", "Flexibility without compromise").
2. Typos and placeholders ("COMPRIMISE", "© Year").
3. Portrait-in-a-box hero with no compositional confidence.
4. Two competing CTAs and a disconnected giant JotForm.
5. Personal Gmail as the business face.

## Category conventions we will honour (top 5)
1. Credentials front-loaded. 2. Three-mode service split. 3. Free low-risk entry. 4. Coach-as-brand bio. 5. Named testimonials with competitive/outcome context.

## Category clichés we will break (top 3)
1. White/static/sans default → dark editorial + crafted motion. 2. Hidden pricing → full transparency. 3. Thin social proof → deep visual proof (before/afters, medals, elite events).

## Contrarian thesis (locked)
RR is the only coach in the category that shows everything — real prices, real before/afters, real competition footage, and a money-back promise — inside a dark, Nike-grade visual frame the rest of the vertical is too timid to attempt.
