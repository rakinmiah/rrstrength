## 1. Category conventions TO HONOUR
1. **Credentials front-loaded** — degrees, coaching levels, competition results stated early (Jason "11 years elite", Character "MSc, 160+ lifters", Team Rohr "World Champion"). The audience buys expertise. *RR: Level 3 PT, 1st-Class BSc, Junior Nationals podium, IPF/SBD-level competitor.*
2. **Three-mode service split** — In-Person / Online / Hybrid is the near-universal structure (Jason, Ellis, Character, Farncombe). Omission confuses the audience.
3. **Free consultation / low-risk entry** — "free initial phone consultation" (Farncombe), email capture (Jason), "I insist on chatting first" (Character). *RR: "Claim Free Session" is on-pattern and stronger.*
4. **Coach-as-the-brand bio** — solo coaches sell the person; an authentic first-person story is expected (Ellis, Character, Jason all centre the coach).
5. **Testimonials with named lifters + competitive context** — the credible sites name names (Jason: 3 named; Character: Ryan F./Paul K. with meet context). *RR has 4 named clients + transformation photos + medals.*

## 2. Category clichés TO BREAK
1. **White background, sans-serif, static** — the entire vertical defaults to it (Jason, Ellis, Character, Farncombe, EasyStrong all white/light, no motion). *RR breaks this with a confident dark editorial aesthetic + crafted scroll motion.*
2. **Hidden pricing** — Jason, Ellis, Character, Team Rohr, EasyStrong all hide price; only Farncombe shows it. Opacity forces a contact-gate and erodes trust. *RR breaks this with full public pricing — tiers + bundles + comparison.*
3. **Thin/absent social proof** — endemic gap (Farncombe, EasyStrong show none; others minimal). *RR breaks this with deep proof: before/afters, medals, hugs, elite-event backdrops.*
4. **Generic motivational filler** — "Lift to your full potential", "Whatever you need to succeed", "grow inside and out of the gym". Interchangeable. *RR breaks this with specific, first-person, mission-anchored copy.*

## 3. Trust-signal matrix (ranked by weight to THIS audience)
1. **Competition pedigree** (national/international placings, IPF/SBD events) — highest; separates a real coach from a gym PT.
2. **Visual results** (before/afters, client medals/trophies) — second; the audience trusts eyes over adjectives. *RR is asset-rich here.*
3. **Formal credentials** (Level 3 PT, BSc, coaching certs) — table stakes for legitimacy.
4. **Named testimonials with outcomes** — high when specific, ignored when vague.
5. **Risk reversal** (money-back / "or you don't pay") — converts fence-sitters; rare and memorable.
6. **Community/charity proof** (events, causes) — differentiator; signals character, builds emotional buy-in. *RR's Ramadan Rumble charity comps.*
7. Instagram/social link — expected, low weight alone.

## 4. Conversion pattern for this vertical
Best-converting flow: **bold benefit hero → instant credibility (credentials + competition) → coach story → clear 3-mode services → visible process → heavy social proof → transparent pricing → low-friction free-trial CTA repeated, with a multi-step form that opens with the easiest question.** The differentiator that lifts conversion here: **transparency** (price + proof shown, not gated) plus **risk reversal** (money-back) — directly attacks the category's trust gap. Form friction is the silent killer — leading with name/goal (not DOB/health) is essential.

## 5. Copy voice brief for this vertical
**Lands:** first-person singular, direct "you", short declaratives, specific numbers, mission-driven warmth, zero jargon.
- **Works:** Character's "measurably improve your total, or you don't pay (Seriously)" (specific + risk reversal + personality); Team Rohr's "Gain Strength, Have Fun, Build Community" (clear values triad); Jason's "I ensure technically excellent lifting with everyone I work with" (concrete promise).
- **Doesn't work:** Ellis's "What I can do for you" (vague); Farncombe's "Whatever you need to succeed" (filler); EasyStrong's "Understanding you and your goals is what drives our coaching decisions" (corporate, no edge).
*RR voice: keep Rashed's authentic first-person ("I got into resistance training… I wanted to look good. The muscle was a byproduct."); cut inherited filler ("Facilitating your growth", "A coach you can trust").*

## 6. Visual aesthetic brief
- **Category default:** white/light, sans-serif, candid photography, generous whitespace, near-zero motion.
- **Award-winning fitness current-year look:** **dark palettes + bold condensed type** (Oswald/Bebas Neue/Montserrat). So RR's bold-uppercase-on-dark is *on-trend — not differentiating by itself*.
- **Dated:** carousels (old RR site, Gymshark uses sparingly), white default, flat thumbnail grids, stock gym imagery.
- **Craft bar (ceiling):** Linear's dark + weight-based hierarchy + asymmetric pairing; Stripe's dense/breathable rhythm + offset spotlights; Nike's full-bleed peak-exertion photography + lower-left offset headline; Apple's centred-focal breaths; Arc's single crafted scroll moment.
- **Implication for RR:** match the on-trend dark/bold floor, then exceed it via (a) asymmetric/offset and full-bleed compositions instead of stacked rectangles, (b) Nike-style full-bleed action photography for the hero, (c) one crafted scroll-linked SIGNATURE moment, (d) disciplined single brick/orange accent.

## 7. Module expectation map
- **Must-have (omission = amateur):** nav, hero with benefit headline + CTA, coach bio, 3-mode services, social proof/testimonials, contact/enquiry, footer.
- **Table-stakes (present in most):** credentials/trust strip, how-it-works process, FAQ.
- **Differentiation opportunities (rare but effective):** transparent pricing with comparison, before/after results gallery, money-back guarantee module, charity/community story, elite-event credibility strip.
- **Anti-patterns (common but harmful):** carousels for services, hidden pricing behind contact, vague motivational hero, single giant intake form before any value is shown.

## 8. The three ceiling references (transferable to RR)
1. **Nike** — full-bleed athletic photography frozen at peak exertion + bold condensed uppercase + lower-left offset headline. RR's hero should be a full-bleed Ramadan-Rumble lift, not a portrait in a box.
2. **Linear** — dark canvas, hierarchy by weight, asymmetric section pairing alternating direction, restrained motion. RR's section system and dark palette discipline.
3. **Stripe** — proven long-scroll rhythm (dense stat ↔ breathable story) and asymmetric coach-spotlight offset. RR's About + Results pacing.

## 9. Contrarian thesis (LOCKED)
**Every competitor hides price and hides behind adjectives; RR wins by being the only one that shows everything — real prices, real before/afters, real competition footage, and a money-back promise — inside a dark, editorial, Nike-grade visual frame the whole category is too timid to attempt.** Defence: the vertical's #1 unmet need is *trust* (every teardown gates price and thins proof); radical transparency + premium craft is the unoccupied corner, and RR is uniquely asset-rich (medals, before/afters, IPF-level footage) to own it.

## 10. Objection map (→ module that resolves it)
1. **"Will this actually work for me / is he legit?"** (trust/competence) → **Trust strip** (credentials + competition) + **Results** (before/afters, medals).
2. **"It's probably expensive / I can't tell what it costs."** (cost/opacity) → **Pricing** (full tiers + bundles + comparison) + **money-back line in Hero**.
3. **"What do I actually get / how does it work?"** (complexity/uncertainty) → **Services** (3 modes) + **How It Works** (6 steps).
4. **"Is he for serious lifters only, or beginners like me?"** (perceived fit) → **About** (Rashed's "I started where you are" story) + **Services** copy spanning beginner→competitor.
5. **"I'm nervous about committing / handing over my details."** (fear of commitment) → **Hero money-back guarantee** + **progressive multi-step form** (easy Step 1) + **FAQ**.
6. **"Can I trust him with my information and is he a real, decent person?"** (trust/character) → **Community & Charity** (Ramadan Rumble) + consent-led form copy.
